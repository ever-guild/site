#### Overall Impression

The first impression is strong: technical, atmospheric, and memorable. The biggest opportunity is not visual polish; it is proof. The site makes senior claims well, but it needs sharper evidence and clearer buyer pathways so a skeptical founder or CTO can trust the claims quickly.

#### What's Working

- The hero is distinctive and on-brand: teal field, gold signal, cream display type, and WebGL atmosphere match "precise, senior, durable."
- Real team photography materially improves trust and prevents the page from feeling like a faceless agency template.
- The contact terminal is memorable and brand-specific; it feels more intentional than a standard contact form block.

#### Priority Issues

**[P1] Proof gap behind senior claims**
Why it matters: The page says "senior," "production-grade," "built to scale," and "software that lasts," but does not show enough concrete proof. High-value buyers need evidence before contact.
Fix: Add a compact proof layer: shipped systems, reliability outcomes, architecture artifacts, rescue examples, audit posture, open-source references, or "what we refuse to ship" principles.
Suggested command: `$impeccable shape proof-layer`

**[P1] Services read like an equal card catalog**
Why it matters: Six same-weight service cards flatten the strategy. Crisis intervention, Web3 engineering, AI automation, and hiring verification are different buyer moments, but the UI treats them equally.
Fix: Reframe services around buyer situations: Build, Stabilize, Scale, Verify. Give each a sharper outcome and one concrete example.
Suggested command: `$impeccable clarify services`

**[P2] Mobile path to contact is too long**
Why it matters: On a 390x844 viewport, Contact starts around 5892px down the page after hero, About, three large team cards, and six service cards. A ready buyer has to scroll too much.
Fix: Add a mid-page contact rail after Team, or compress service detail on mobile with tighter summaries.
Suggested command: `$impeccable adapt mobile-contact-path`

**[P2] Visible external channel coverage is incomplete**
Why it matters: Project guidance says Telegram must be maintained, but visible social/contact surfaces show GitHub, LinkedIn, X, and Upwork without Telegram. This creates a requirements mismatch.
Fix: Add Telegram wherever social/contact channels appear, or update the project guidance if Telegram is no longer part of the public surface.
Suggested command: `$impeccable polish contact-channels`

**[P3] Design-system documentation misses implementation literals**
Why it matters: The detector flags undocumented `#000`, `rgba(0, 22, 29, 0.97)`, `2px`, `6px`, and `8px` values. They are likely legitimate masks/focus/scrollbar details, but undocumented literals create future drift.
Fix: Add mask, overlay, focus micro-radius, and scrollbar radius notes to `DESIGN.md` / `.impeccable/design.json`, or replace literals with existing tokens where appropriate.
Suggested command: `$impeccable document`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Jordan, first-time founder evaluating fit**: Jordan understands that Ever Guild is senior and technical, but cannot quickly tell whether the right path is greenfield build, rescue, maintenance, or audit. The service grid gives categories, not guidance.

**Sam, skeptical engineering leader**: Sam will like the senior tone but will look for evidence: shipped systems, production incidents handled, audit posture, client outcomes, architecture depth. The current page asks Sam to infer too much.

**Casey, urgent project owner in crisis**: Casey sees "Crisis Engineering Management," but it appears as card 6 in an equal grid and has no emergency route. There is no "project in trouble?" path and no visible fallback channel beyond email.

**Accessibility-dependent user**: Hit areas measure at least 44px and focus states are visible. The remaining risk is not basic keyboard/tap access; it is mailto dependency and icon-only social recognition.
